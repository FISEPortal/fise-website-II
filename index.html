The Problem

You don't own
yourself online.

Every app, every platform, every AI tool you use was built on infrastructure that — by design — keeps your identity, your health data, your behavioral patterns, and your financial value locked inside someone else's system. That's not a bug. It's the business model.

AI, BCI, and Neurotech are generating the most sensitive data humanity has ever produced: the electrical activity of your mind, the biometric signature of your body, the neural patterns that reveal what you think and feel — before you've consciously decided any of it.

Two-thirds of neurotechnology companies are already sharing or selling this data with third parties. The vast majority don't disclose where it's stored, how long they keep it, who has access, or what happens if there's a breach.

And you cannot safely or ethically build the next generation of AI and Neurotech on a foundation built to extract rather than elevate.

"Safety and ethics are only as strong as the infrastructure beneath them. Policy without protocol is aspiration without proof."

BCI devices generate continuous neural data, thought patterns, cognitive states, motor intentions. Today, that data belongs to the device manufacturer. With FISE, it belongs to you: owned, monetized on your terms, and legally protected from misuse.

Neurotech & Wearables

From EEG headbands to biosensors, the consumer Neurotech market is expanding rapidly, with virtually no data governance beneath it. FISE provides the protocol layer that Neurotech companies need to operate compliantly, and that humans need to participate safely.

Biotech & Health Data

Genomic data, biometrics, and health records are among the most valuable, and most exploited, data classes in existence. FISE's sovereignty architecture applies directly to biotech, enabling pharmaceutical research, clinical trials, and health data markets to operate with verifiable human consent and enforceable terms.

As AI agents act on behalf of humans, negotiating, transacting, making decisions, the identity and data governance layer beneath them becomes critical. FISE's human master DID architecture governs AI agents directly, ensuring every agent action traces back to verifiable human consent.
